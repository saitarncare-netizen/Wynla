Direction A — Y-fork (trail signpost) ⭐ Recommended

Concept: The Y in "wynla" forms a natural fork. Fork = where trails diverge = the moment of choosing where to ski = the brand promise (plan smart). The center stem grounds it. Gold dot at the fork marks the decision point.

Why this: Unique to Wynla (W is generic; Y is rare in tech). Geometric like Linear/Vercel/Notion. Direct symbol for "plan a trip" — no other ski app uses this metaphor.

Pros

  • Unique metaphor: fork = planning a trip (matches brand pillar #1)
  • Y is rare in tech logos → ownable
  • Geometric and clean → matches Linear / Notion / Vercel reference set
  • Works as standalone mark (favicon) AND with wordmark
  • Gold "trail marker" dot adds personality without cliché

Cons

  • Some viewers won't decode "Y = fork = planning" on first look
  • Could read as a "person with arms up" if drawn too cheerful
  • Less directly ski-related (no mountain / snow visible)

Direction B — Single peak with snow cap Alternative

Concept: One clean geometric peak (not the M-shape range we had before). Gold foreground peak + subtle sky-blue background peak for depth. White triangle snow cap on the summit. Direct ski-association without being a snowflake or a stock mountain.

Why this: Most immediately readable as "ski / outdoors". Iconic single-peak silhouettes (Vercel ▲) prove this can be ownable. Two-tone depth lifts it above generic mountain logos.

Direction C — Pure wordmark Alternative

Concept: "wynla." in clean geometric lowercase. The period in brand gold = "planned. done." No icon, no mountain. Pure typography — let the name carry it (Stripe / Linear school).

Why this: Most mature, sophisticated. Hardest to do badly because typography is binary — either right or wrong. Pairs beautifully with the existing Geist font system already in the app.

  • Pairs natively with the Geist font already in the app
  • Scales beautifully — typography always sharp
  • Honest: lets the brand name itself be the brand

Cons

  • No iconic mark for favicon → need to adapt "w." monogram
  • Less memorable than a symbol (Y-fork or peak)
  • Riskier if competitors release strong icons later
